Schooling

I gratuated from ISBS Paris (Health and Biomedical engineering) (now EPISEN), in 2018 with bioinformatics/imaging and biomechanics/biomaterial specialities. In addition to the medical biology courses, this engineering school provides skills in all areas related to health, such as computer science, marketing, economics, regulation and quality, mechanics etc. Half of the week was spent at the Faculty of Medicine in Créteil (UPEC) for courses in medical biology, mechanics, biomaterials and regulations mainly. The other half took place at ESIEE Marne-la-Vallée for courses in computer science, algorithms, marketing, management etc. This school therefore enabled me to acquire knowledge in all areas related to health. This multidisciplinarity allows a good adaptation when entering the job market and to be able to interact with the different teams of a company. It also gives a global vision of the development of medical products. However, although specialised in biomedical, the skills acquired can also be used in any other field.

Current personal projects

Application to learn a new language, Android Studio

I am currently working on an Android application to help to learn a language. It is a dictionary of learned words that we can add by ourself. It contains tests on these words. We can classify the words by theme, importance, degree of learning etc. and choose only some of them for the test. I began this application, first to learn Java but also because, when I was learning a language (Swedish) I found a lot of application that test you to learn words but non of them kept track of all learned words and grammar rules. So I decided to create my own Android application.
Acquired knowledge: Android studio, Java, HTML, CSS, XML, SQLite
