Ludivine Morvan

I am a PhD student working in computer-aided prognosis with medical images.

In this website you will find my CV and publications but most importantly, tutorials about different aspects of my work such as image processing, deep learning, and survival analysis.

I gratuated from ISBS Paris (Health and Biomedical engineering), in 2018 with bioinformatics/imaging and biomechanics/biomaterial specialities. You can find more details about my schooling, internships, and teaching experiences on my CV page.

Current work

I am currently working as PhD student at CRCINA (Centre de Recherche en Cancerologie et Immunologie Nantes Angers), INSERM, NANTES and at the LS2N (Laboratoire des Sciences du Num√©rique de Nantes) in the SIMS team since March 2018. The purpose of my thesis is to do survival analysis and find biomarkers of multiple myeloma, a bone marrow cancer, using clinical data, PET images, and in the future CT-scans. Data comes from the prospective and multicentric IMAJEM study [Moreau et al., 2017]

I first worked with the Random Survival Forests (RSF) [Ishwaran et al.,2008] and radiomics to predict the progression free survival of patients and find relevent biomarkers [Morvan et al., 2019].

Now, I am adapting deep learning to survival analysis. Some works exist but none of them produce good results, especially with small PET images of lesions.
